Presentation Elly Martin: Equipment group

Equipment group: update and future plans. We want to make sure that we are doing things that are interesting for everyone. What we have done so far:
draft recommendations for acoustic output calibration, testing, and routine checks of equipment for transcranial focused ultrasound for neuromodulation. The aim was to:

  • - Help users ensure equipment output is stable and known
  • - Present suggestions for how to do this
  • - Make it accessible to all users

Our first document was published on the ITRUSST website in September:

  1. primary calibration, to know what your source is and link from the power that you set and the acoustic focus. E.g. from manufacturer.
  2. secondary calibration/testing – eg. In house simple measurements
  3. routine equipment checks – eg. Based on electrical quantities, before study sessions. Still to come – some detailed recommendations and examples of how to check consistency of output regularly.

These are the main things we thought about so far.

Safe operation of equipment: What useful safety features could manufacturers usefully and feasibly implement?

  • - Two step output verification
  • - Information displayed during use
  • - Log files
  • - Emergency stop
  • - Standard test reports provided to user (recommendations what should be in there)
  • - Thermal/electrical monitoring? ( to make sure non off the channels are broken)

Future activities: what should we think about?

  • - Guidance or summary of standard measurement procedures for acquiring quantities recommended to be reported? -> eg not super easy to read and not accessible for everyone
    • * Standardised reporting group
  • - What information about the source that is needed in order to get useful field prediction from stimulation?
    • * Planning group
  • - What information about the source should be reported?
    • * Safety group

Elly Martin: I would love to hear if people think it’s useful, tweaking of completely different things where we could think about.
